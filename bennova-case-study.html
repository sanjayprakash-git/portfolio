Executive Summary

141|141|

This document presents a full business analysis engagement conducted at a Dublin-based retail and e-commerce SME operating a proprietary POS and inventory management system. The business sells accessories, gadgets, phones, and laptops, and provides phone and laptop repair services — managing over 1,000 active SKUs including retail stock and repair parts.

142|142|

A process review of the end-to-end stock management workflow identified six critical process gaps causing systematic inventory inaccuracies, unrecorded sales, phantom stock, and unreliable cost-of-goods data across the business.

143|143| 144|144|
145|145| Core Finding 146|146| The current process relies on manual intervention at four critical control points — stock monitoring, reorder placement, end-of-day reconciliation, and refurbishment job costing — none of which are integrated with the system. This creates a compounding data quality problem where inventory counts cannot be trusted at any point, reorder decisions are made on incomplete information, and cash discrepancies are resolved by patching inventory rather than correcting the root cause. 147|147|
148|148| 149|149|
150|150| Recommendation 151|151| Proceed with targeted process and configuration fixes within the existing system before evaluating a platform migration. Four of the six gaps can be resolved through system configuration, process enforcement, and staff training at a total cost of approximately €150. The two remaining gaps (automated alerts and Bill of Materials for refurb jobs) require system capability assessment — if unsupported, RepairDesk (€75/month) is the recommended platform given its repair + retail + inventory combination. 152|152|

Section 5

Business Requirements Specification

The following requirements are derived directly from the six identified gaps. Each requirement is written in "The system shall..." format and includes measurable acceptance criteria suitable for UAT verification.

Req IDGap RefTypeBusiness RequirementAcceptance Criteria
BR-01GAP-3Functional The system shall generate an automated low stock alert when any product reaches or falls below a configurable reorder threshold Alert delivered to manager dashboard and email within 60 seconds of threshold breach. Threshold configurable per SKU. No manual check required to trigger alert.
BR-02GAP-2Functional The system shall allow managers to raise a Purchase Order directly within the system, selecting supplier, product, quantity, and expected delivery date PO created, timestamped, and assigned a unique reference number. Supplier notified via system-generated email. PO visible in inventory module with status: Open / In Transit / Received.
BR-03GAP-1Functional The system shall block sale completion when a product barcode is not found and shall not permit manual name-based product selection as a fallback Unrecognised barcode triggers a product creation prompt. Sale cannot proceed until a valid barcode is assigned. Free-text product selection not available to staff during billing.
BR-04GAP-4Functional The system shall prevent End of Day close if unrecorded transactions are detected, requiring all transactions to be completed or explicitly voided before EOD is finalised EOD close button disabled if cash variance exceeds configurable tolerance (default: €0). System prompts staff to record outstanding transactions. Manual stock adjustment to balance cash register not permitted.
BR-05GAP-5Functional The system shall require a Purchase Order to exist and be in Open status before a delivery can be received and inventory updated Goods receipt module only accessible for products linked to an open PO. Inventory update triggers automatic PO status change to Received. Retrospective PO creation not permitted after goods receipt.
BR-06GAP-6Functional The system shall require parts used in any repair or refurbishment job to be selected from inventory SKUs and automatically deducted from stock on job completion Job ticket must include a parts list linked to inventory SKUs before job can be set to Complete. Stock count reduces automatically on job completion. Manual stock adjustment for repair parts not permitted outside job ticket workflow.
NFR-01AllNon-Functional Stock level updates shall reflect in the system within 60 seconds of any sale, goods receipt, return, or job completion being processed System availability 99.9% during trading hours (09:00–21:00). Update latency not to exceed 60 seconds under normal load. System must support minimum 1,000 active SKUs without performance degradation.

Conclusion The investment is minimal — primarily staff time and a single training session. The benefit is disproportionate: reliable inventory data across 1,000+ SKUs, recovered manager time, accurate cost-of-goods on refurbished devices, and a complete audit trail for all stock movements. This is a process discipline problem, not a technology problem — the technology already exists in the current system and is not being used correctly.
